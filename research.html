SRF-model

Ice Accretion Simulation with Roughness Effects and Separation Correction

Physics of Fluids 2024 Editor's Pick Aerospace Science and Technology IJHFF 2026

Accurate prediction of ice accretion on airfoils and wings requires capturing the interplay between surface roughness, boundary-layer transition, and flow separation—three coupled phenomena that standard RANS models struggle with simultaneously. This work develops a unified simulation framework that (1) implements an equivalent sand-grain roughness model to reproduce the drag penalty of ice-roughened surfaces, (2) embeds a flow-separation correction into a modified k-ω-γ transition model to recover the laminar separation bubble beneath the ice horn, and (3) couples both modifications with an ice accretion solver to produce physically consistent ice shapes on 2-D airfoils and 3-D swept wings with both continuous and discontinuous ice.

Validations against NACA 23012 and NLF-0414 experiments demonstrate that the corrected transition model reduces lift-coefficient error by up to 60 % under severe icing conditions compared with the baseline SST k-ω model, while correctly reproducing the double-horn ice geometry that governs stall behavior.

Generative Learning for Unsteady Aerodynamics over Iced Airfoils

In Preparation

Predicting unsteady, stochastic flow-separation dynamics over ice-contaminated airfoils is prohibitively expensive with high-fidelity CFD alone. This project develops a Transformer-diffusion generative framework that learns the conditional distribution of unsteady aerodynamic fields from a library of LES/DNS snapshots and can produce statistically faithful realizations at a fraction of the cost, enabling rapid uncertainty quantification for icing certification.

Manuscript in preparation — figures and results will be posted upon publication.

03

PhysMiner: Large Language Model for Physics Discovery in Turbulence

In Preparation

Turbulence modeling has long relied on expert intuition to propose closure hypotheses. PhysMiner is an LLM-driven framework that autonomously mines the fluid mechanics literature, generates physically interpretable subgrid-scale hypotheses, and validates them against DNS databases—closing the loop between language-grounded physics reasoning and numerical simulation. An early application demonstrates the discovery of novel subgrid length-scale expressions for Detached Eddy Simulation that outperform hand-crafted formulations on separated flows.
