About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at Iowa State University, Ames, IA. My research is centered on turbulence modeling, aerodynamic icing simulation, and the integration of scientific machine learning with computational fluid dynamics.

I received my Ph.D. in Mechanics from the School of Aerospace Engineering, Tsinghua University (2024). Before joining Iowa State University, I was a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the Department of Mathematics, University of Texas at Arlington (2024–2025), where I conducted large eddy simulations and developed Liutex-based vortex identification methods.

Only a profound understanding of the underlying physics of turbulence can pave the way for truly effective turbulence modeling.

I serve as a reviewer for Physics of Fluids, Aerospace Science and Technology, and International Journal of Heat and Fluid Flow.
